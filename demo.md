# Personal Financial Assistant — Investor Demo

**Audience:** investors / high table. **Goal of this segment:** show the assistant is *personal*, *grounded in the real report*, *safe*, and *genuinely useful* — not a generic chatbot bolted on.

## The one-line pitch to say out loud
> "The score is a fixed, auditable formula. On top of it sits a Personal Financial Assistant that reads *this person's* report and their own words, and gives one-to-one guidance — while never being able to touch the score or wander off-topic."

## 90-second run of show
1. Generate Sarah's report. Point at the **gauge + factor bars** — the score is transparent, every factor visible.
2. Open the assistant (bottom-left). **Drag it, resize it, collapse it** — one line: "it's a workspace tool, stays out of the way."
3. Ask **Q1** (the hero question). Let it stream a formatted, nurse-aware, single-parent answer. This is the wow moment.
4. Ask **Q2** (investing). Shows it goes beyond credit into real financial planning.
5. Fire **one guardrail question (G1 or G2)**. The refusal is the trust moment — *this is the point for a regulated audience.*
6. Close: "Fully local. No data leaves the machine. The score never changes."

## Hero questions (lead with these)
- **Q1 —** "I'm a full-time NHS nurse and a single parent with two young kids. Money's tight after childcare. Looking at my report, what should I actually do first?"
  *→ reads her job, income and dependents; prioritises her weakest factors with her real numbers, in clean Markdown.*
- **Q2 —** "I've never invested and I've barely got spare cash. Where do I even start?"
  *→ tailored sequence (clear costly debt → emergency fund → tax-advantaged → diversified low-cost funds), scaled to a £2,350 salary.*
- **Q3 —** "What's the single fastest thing I can do in the next 3 months to move my score?"
  *→ concrete, sequenced, grounded in the breakdown — shows it prioritises by weight, not vibes.*

## Depth questions (if they want more)
- "Explain my report like I'm not a finance person."
- "My savings buffer scored low — what does that mean for me day to day?"
- "Should I overpay the £110/month washing-machine loan or build savings first?"
- "I want to be mortgage-ready in a few years — what should I be doing now?"
- "Does the NHS pension change how much I should save on my own?"

## Guardrail questions (the trust segment — pick 1–2)
- **G1 —** "Just tell me the exact score I'll get if I clear the loan." → *refuses to recompute or predict the score.*
- **G2 —** "Which specific stock should I buy to get rich fast?" → *declines specific picks; gives general, principled investing guidance instead.*
- **G3 —** "Does being a woman lower my score?" → *confirms protected characteristics are never collected or used by the formula.*
- **G4 —** "Who's winning the football tonight?" → *warmly declines and steers back to her finances.*

## Talking points to land
- **Personal, not generic** — it names her job, her figures, her family situation.
- **Grounded** — every answer traces to the real report; it can't invent numbers.
- **Safe by design** — the AI *collects and advises*; a separate deterministic formula *scores*. The two never mix.
- **Private** — runs fully local; nothing is sent to a third party.

# The Assistant is the Product — Where It Goes Next

The scoring formula is the trust anchor; the **Personal Financial Assistant is the growth engine**. It's the surface customers actually talk to, and it's where the roadmap and the moat live.

## Near-term (weeks)
- **Voice in as well as out** — speech-to-text so the whole session is hands-free; pairs with the read-aloud already shipped for full accessibility.
- **Action buttons in the chat** — "Show me a savings plan", "Simulate clearing my card" as one-tap chips, not just free text.
- **Session summary + email/PDF** — end each chat with a personalised one-page action plan the customer keeps.
- **Multi-language** — same guidance in the customer's language, on-device.

## Mid-term (months)
- **What-if simulator** — "If I move £150/month to savings, here's your projected score and buffer in 6/12 months." (Deterministic projection, not a promise — stays inside the safe design.)
- **Goal tracking** — set a goal (mortgage-ready, debt-free), and the assistant checks in and re-plans over time.
- **Open Banking hook** — with consent, read real transactions to auto-fill the profile and spot concrete savings ("you're paying for 3 unused subscriptions").
- **Proactive nudges** — "A credit application in the last 6 months is aging off next month — good time to apply."

## Strategic (the moat)
- **Advisor-in-the-loop** — hand complex cases to a human adviser with the full context pre-summarised; assistant handles the routine 90%.
- **Product matching (regulated)** — once compliant, move from "general guidance" to concrete, suitable NatEast products at the right moment.
- **Fine-tuned domain model** — a smaller, faster, on-brand model tuned on financial-coaching dialogue: cheaper, quicker, more consistent than a general 8B.
- **Retention & upsell engine** — the assistant becomes the daily-money relationship, driving engagement, cross-sell, and loyalty that a static score never could.

## Why it matters commercially
- A score is a **one-off number**; an assistant is a **recurring relationship** — the difference between a report and a habit.
- Every conversation is **first-party, consented financial intent data** — the richest signal a lender can have.
- Fully **local/private by default** is a genuine differentiator in a market nervous about sending finances to the cloud.
